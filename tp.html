Uttarakhand Tourism Policy

Promoting sustainable and inclusive tourism in the land of natural beauty and spirituality.

Introduction

Welcome to the Uttarakhand Tourism Policy page, your gateway to understanding the visionary framework shaping tourism in the "Land of Gods." Uttarakhand, known for its serene landscapes, spiritual heritage, and adventure opportunities, has established a comprehensive policy to enhance its appeal as a global tourism destination. The Tourism Policy of Uttarakhand focuses on sustainable development, community involvement, and eco-friendly practices, ensuring that tourism contributes positively to the environment, local livelihoods, and cultural preservation. This policy aims to provide a seamless experience for travelers while fostering economic growth and conserving the state's natural and cultural wealth. Whether you are a nature enthusiast, adventure seeker, or spiritual traveler, the Uttarakhand Tourism Policy strives to make the state a welcoming and enriching destination for all. Explore this page to learn about the policy's objectives, key highlights, and the initiatives undertaken to transform Uttarakhand into a global tourism hub.

Objectives of the Policy

The Uttarakhand Tourism Policy is designed with a vision to establish the state as a leading global tourism destination while ensuring sustainable and inclusive growth. The policy emphasizes the promotion of sustainable tourism by preserving Uttarakhand’s rich natural environment, biodiversity, and cultural heritage for future generations. A key focus is on enhancing infrastructure and accessibility, improving connectivity to remote areas through road, rail, and air networks, and providing world-class tourism facilities to ensure seamless travel experiences. Empowering local communities is at the core of the policy, aiming to generate employment opportunities and support local entrepreneurship. By encouraging eco-tourism practices, the policy seeks to minimize environmental impact and foster the conservation of Uttarakhand’s pristine ecosystems. Additionally, it aspires to position the state as a hub for adventure tourism by supporting activities such as trekking, skiing, river rafting, and mountaineering, while also promoting spiritual and cultural tourism through enhanced facilities at pilgrimage sites like Rishikesh, Haridwar, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. The policy promotes innovative tourism models such as village tourism, homestays, and wellness tourism, offering visitors unique and authentic experiences. It prioritizes the safety and security of tourists by implementing robust regulatory frameworks and emergency response systems. By fostering public-private partnerships (PPP), the policy encourages investments and expertise in the tourism sector to achieve balanced growth. Furthermore, the policy focuses on increasing Uttarakhand’s global visibility through targeted marketing and active participation in international tourism events, ensuring that the state becomes a preferred destination for travelers worldwide.
