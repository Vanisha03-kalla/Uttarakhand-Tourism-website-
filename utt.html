DISCOVER UTTRAKHAND

Mussoorie

Mussoorie, nestled in Uttarakhand's Garhwal Himalayas, is a scenic hill station known for its panoramic views, colonial architecture, and popular spots like Gun Hill and Kempty Falls. Often called the "Queen of the Hills," it's a perfect retreat for nature lovers.

Dehradun

Dehradun, the capital city of Uttarakhand, is nestled between the Ganges and Yamuna rivers in the Doon Valley. Known for its pleasant climate and scenic landscapes, it serves as a gateway to the hill stations of Mussoorie and the pilgrimage sites of Haridwar and Rishikesh. The city is also home to prestigious educational institutions, like the Doon School and the Indian Military Academy.

Nainital

Nainital is a charming hill station in Uttarakhand, famous for its emerald-green lake and picturesque surroundings. Situated at an altitude of approximately 2,000 meters, it offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas. The town is dotted with colonial-era buildings, bustling markets, and serene spots like Naina Devi Temple and Snow View Point.

Read More

Rishikesh

Rishikesh, located in the foothills of the Himalayas along the banks of the Ganges River, is renowned as the "Yoga Capital of the World." It is a spiritual hub, attracting yoga enthusiasts, pilgrims, and adventure seekers alike. Rishikesh is also known for its ashrams, temples, and the iconic Laxman Jhula suspension bridge.

Read More

Haridwar

Haridwar is one of India's holiest cities, situated on the banks of the Ganges River. It is a major pilgrimage site where millions of devotees gather for the Kumbh Mela and daily Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri. The city's spiritual ambiance and historical significance make it a must-visit destination for those seeking divine blessings and cultural experiences.

Read More

Chamoli

Chamoli, in Uttarakhand's Garhwal region, is a district known for its natural beauty and spiritual significance. It's home to important pilgrimage sites like Badrinath and offers stunning landscapes ideal for trekking and nature lovers.
